Kenya general Elections Finally commences

  • Jeff Koinange
  • Aug 9, 2022
  • 12 Comments

Kenya is a democratic country that holds elections every five years to executeives to represent the people from different levels: president, governor, women represenative, senator, member of parliament and member of county assembly. On this day kenyans are mandated to exercise their democratic rights through a secret ballot which is head Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission.

The exercise is to be ceasing by 5pm on this day thereafter tallying of the votes commences to determine the wiiner of different seats.

Every vote counts. Vote wisely Vote Future!

The nationals are optimistic that whoever they'll vote in will serve them creditably for the next five years. Upon the end of tallying and declaration of the winner, the unasatisfied parties and candidates are therefore to file a petition opposing the same within specified time as outlined by the law. Incase, of no complain, the executive is sworn in to resume the office. We hope that this will be free, fair and credible elections.
